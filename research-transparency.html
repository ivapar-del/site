Research / I.

Transparency, Regulation & Conflict-of-Interest Assessment

This strand tracks how transparency rules are designed, implemented, and enforced across regulatory and institutional contexts — with a focus on conflicts of interest in healthcare, political finance disclosure, and the governance of expert advisory processes.

Conflicts of interest in healthcare regulation

A core concern in this work is the undisclosed financial relationship between pharmaceutical industry and the bodies that evaluate and recommend drugs: patient organisations, clinical assessment panels, and health technology assessment agencies. I have documented these relationships in the UK (NICE), across the EU (Joint Clinical Assessment), and in France, producing systematic evidence of how undisclosed ties distort regulatory decisions.

This work was published in BMJ and BMJ Open and has contributed to policy debates at the European Commission and the UK's NICE. It draws on freedom-of-information requests, financial disclosure databases, and retrospective document analysis.

Industry funding of civil society organisations

A connected line of research examines the funding of patient organisations by pharmaceutical companies in the UK and internationally. Using novel datasets on declared funding relationships, I map the concentration of industry money, its correlation with disease prevalence and drug spending, and its implications for the independence of patient advocacy at regulatory hearings.

Political finance transparency in Europe

In collaboration with LUISS Guido Carli and the European Research Center for Anti-Corruption and State-Building (ERCAS), I am working on a comparative assessment of political finance disclosure regimes across EU member states. The project examines variation in legal requirements, enforcement capacity, and the integrity of disclosure data, and feeds into EU-level assessments of corruption risk and democratic governance.

Corruption risk assessment — European Commission

I contributed to a major ERCAS report prepared for the European Commission on the causes and trends of corruption risk in Europe (Working Paper 66, 2025), covering institutional diagnostics across member states and candidate countries. This work applies a fact-based assessment methodology that is increasingly used by multilateral organisations and national anti-corruption agencies.

Research / II.

Public Procurement, Supply Chains & Integrity

This strand uses administrative data and quantitative methods to study corruption risk in public procurement — from contract award decisions to the integrity of pharmaceutical supply chains.

Favouritism and corruption in public procurement

Public procurement accounts for around 14% of GDP in OECD countries and is one of the most corruption-prone areas of public administration. My work in this area uses large administrative datasets — including EU-level contract award databases and national public spending records — to identify patterns of irregularity consistent with favouritism: single-bidder contracts, short submission windows, and the concentration of awards among politically connected firms.

This research feeds into both academic debates on state capture and practical tools used by anti-corruption agencies and the European Commission to flag high-risk procurement processes in real time.

Supply chain integrity in healthcare

In collaboration with Transparency International, I am examining corruption risk in the supply chain of generic antibiotics — a sector characterised by complex multi-country sourcing, limited regulatory oversight in origin countries, and a documented problem of counterfeit and falsified medicines. The project maps the structural features of supply chains that create vulnerability to corruption and identifies regulatory leverage points for intervention.

Corruption and inequality

A related project, in collaboration with LUISS Guido Carli, examines whether and how corruption increases economic inequality. Using fact-based assessment data across EU member states, we exploit institutional variation to identify a causal pathway from corruption in public resource allocation to divergent household outcomes. This work contributes to longstanding debates on the distributional consequences of governance failures.

Anti-corruption interventions — evidence synthesis

Together with colleagues at the London School of Hygiene and Tropical Medicine, I am conducting a systematic review of the evidence on administrative anti-corruption interventions in healthcare — covering procurement regulation, transparency requirements, whistleblower protections, and audit mechanisms. The review aims to provide a rigorous evidence base for policymakers and international organisations designing anti-corruption programmes.

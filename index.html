I am an applied economist with experience spanning academic research, policy advisory work, and institutional collaboration. My research has been published in Governance, the British Medical Journal,Social Science and Medicine, BMJ Open. It has been covered by The Guardian and the BBC. I work as an external expert for the UN Department of Economic and Social Affairs, the World Bank, Transparency International, and the WHO's Lancet Commission on Corruption in Healthcare. I currently work on the Horizon Europe project BridgeGap at LUISS Guido Carli.

I hold a PhD from the London School of Economics and Political Science, where my dissertation examined governance in healthcare, and specifically the interplay between corruption and social norms.
